The history of tea leaf in the world

All tea comes from the tropical plant known as Camellia Sinensis. The tea plant grows best in a warm climate with long sunlit days, cool nights, and an abundance of rainfall. Tea plants grow at altitudes ranging from sea level to 7,000 feet and on latitudes as far north as Turkey and as far south as Argentina.

The best tea grows at higher altitudes and many bushes can be cultivated for over 100 years. Tea bushes cover about six million acres of the earth and are harvested every week during the almost year-long growing season.
